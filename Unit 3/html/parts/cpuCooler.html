CPU Cooler

The CPU cooler required will greatly depend on the CPU that you purchase. Most CPUs will come with an included cooler, which should be more then enough for the typical user. If you would like to overclock, then an aftermarket cooler would be beneficial as it can provide more cooling. There are 2 main types of coolers, air coolers, and water coolers.

Air Coolers work by having heat pipes directly contact the CPU, and move the heat to a fin array. The multiple fins within the fin array disperse the heat over a large surface area. There are fans that move the heat away from the fins, cooling the CPU.

Water Coolers work by having a place attached to the CPU. Water flows from the plate to a radiator, transferring heat. Fans blow air on the radiator cooling the liquid, similar to cooling a fin array. The water is flowed constantly, keeping the water temperature cool, and cooling the CPU.
