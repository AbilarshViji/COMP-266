Storage

Storage comes in 2 formats, SATA and M.2. SATA drives are 2.5" SSDs and 3.5" hard drives. They go into specific slots in your case to mount. They require a SATA power connector from the PSU, and a SATA data connector which connects the motherboard to the drive. SATA is a slower protocol, so the drives are limited in the speeds they can achieve.

M.2 drives connect directly to the motherboard. There is a slot which the drive connects to. There are no separate connectors required. M.2 drives support a protocol called NVMe. NVMe uses PCIe for the drive connection protocol instead of SATA. This allows for much faster speeds on the drive.
